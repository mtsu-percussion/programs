Program

Tap a piece to access program notes.
  • Shift
    David England
    (b. 1970)
    conducted by Christian Kilgore
    David England's Shift was composed for the Blackman Middle School Percussion Ensemble's showcase concert at PASIC 2024 in Indianapolis, IN. The title refers to the frequent shifts in meter, dynamics, timbre, and lead voice that are featured throughout the piece.
  • Curious Surroundings
    Clif Walker
    (b. 1972)
    Commissioned by Dr. Adam Groh and the Western Carolina University Percussion Ensemble, Curious Surroundings is inspired by the folklore of the North Carolina mountains. The piece is divided into three connected sections, each based on a different well-known ghost story from the area:

    I. The Phantom Choir of Roan Mountain

    II. The Brown Mountain Lights from Wiseman's View

    III. The Demon Dog of Valle Crucis
  • Blur
    Cesar Gonzalez
    (b. 1991)
    conducted by Samuel Brooke
    Cesar Gonzalez's Blur was commissioned by Colton Bean and the Winston Churchill High School Percussion Ensemble, and was premiered at the 2019 Midwest Band and Orchestra Clinic in Chicago, IL. The piece is designed to be colorful and rhythmically driving, with rhythmic motifs and flourishes of harmony and texture creating a blur of musical sonorities.
  • Aftershock
    Alan Keown
    (b. 1957)
    featuring the 2025
    Percussion Techniques Class
    Aftershock was written as a sequel to Keown's Shockwave. The piece makes use of many fundamental techniques, and develops a simple rhythmic motif throughout. Aftershock was originally written for eight percussionists, but we have greatly expanded the personnel to involve the entire 2025 Percussion Techniques class!
  • Persistence
    Brian Blume
    (b. 1985)
    Inspired in part by the sounds and rhythms of Trent Reznor, Brian Blume's Persistence is written for five performers in a compact setup involving the sharing of several instruments. The title is in reference to the steady 16th-note rhythm played on the metal pipe, which persistently moves the music forward despite frequent changes in meter and accents. The piece was composed in the spring of 2015 and was premiered at the McCormick Marimba Festival at the University of South Florida in Tampa on January 30, 2016.
  • Ritual Music
    David Skidmore
    (b. 1982)
    featuring the graduate students of the MTSU Percussion Studio
    David Skidmore's Ritual Music is a highly energetic and at times bombastic percussion quartet, composed originally for a dance troupe and premiered by Third Coast Percussion in 2004. Driving rhythms and creative timbral combinations make this piece an exciting experience for performers and audience alike.
  • Together At Last
    Lalo Davila
    (b. 1956)
    directed by Angel Soto
    Together At Last was originally composed for eight performers featuring a marimba soloist. It was composed for the inaugural TMEA All-State 5A Percussion Ensemble. The title of this work references the time for which TMEA did not occur due to Covid-19. Composed in the Folkloric style, Together At Last also features the cajon and is accompanied by traditional hand clappers. Exciting to listen to and fun to perform!
  • The First Circle
    Pat Metheny,
    (b. 1954)
    arr. McDonald/
    Kingan
    featuring
    Sydnei Humphrey, vocals
    David Guydon, piano
    Pat Metheny's The First Circle was the title track to the Pat Metheny Group's fourth studio album, released in 1984. This percussion ensemble arrangement was completed in January of 1987 and does an amazing job of translating the beautiful colors of the original recording into the realm of the percussion ensemble. We are thrilled to include Sydnei Humphrey and David Guydon on this performance.
